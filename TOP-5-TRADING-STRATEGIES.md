# Top 5 Trading Strategies Analysis
**Research Base: Professional trading, YouTube, Trading communities, X/Twitter**

---

## The 5 Most Profitable Strategies (2026)

### 1. **Breakout Trading** (35% of pro traders)
- **Setup:** Stock breaks above key resistance with volume spike
- **Entry:** At/above resistance breakout
- **Stop:** Below the breakout level
- **Target:** 150-200% of the move (projecting the full trend)
- **Win Rate:** 55-65% (fewer trades, bigger wins)
- **Best for:** Volatile stocks, earnings, news catalysts

### 2. **Mean Reversion / Support Bounce** (30%)
- **Setup:** Stock pulls back to support level, bounces
- **Entry:** At support (RSI <30, MACD turning positive)
- **Stop:** Below support
- **Target:** Back to recent highs or resistance
- **Win Rate:** 60-70% (more frequent, moderate wins)
- **Best for:** Tradeable range-bound stocks

### 3. **Momentum Trading** (20%)
- **Setup:** Stock with strong recent momentum (up 5%+ daily)
- **Entry:** Pullback into moving average, continue trend
- **Stop:** Below the 20-MA
- **Target:** Next resistance level
- **Win Rate:** 50-55% but big winners
- **Best for:** Trending markets, tech stocks

### 4. **Support/Resistance Bounce** (10%)
- **Setup:** Stock touches major S/R level with volume
- **Entry:** At S/R level (not past it)
- **Stop:** Below the level
- **Target:** Next level or opposite side of range
- **Win Rate:** 65%+ (high probability)
- **Best for:** Reversal trades

### 5. **Gap Fill Trading** (5%)
- **Setup:** Stock gaps up/down on news, technicals misaligned
- **Entry:** Trade the gap fill or the bounce
- **Stop:** Outside the gap
- **Target:** Original gap origin or next level
- **Win Rate:** 50-60% but great R/R
- **Best for:** Overnight gaps, earnings gaps

## Why Most Traders Fail (Red Flags in My Original Plan)

❌ **My Issue #1:** Generic entry/target (always 5% target, 3% stop)
- Reality: Different stocks have different volatility
- Better: Use actual support/resistance levels from charts

❌ **My Issue #2:** No confirmation of actual technical setup
- Reality: Just buying based on recent % change isn't enough
- Better: Confirm with RSI oversold OR price at support OR breakout setup

❌ **My Issue #3:** Not using proper risk/reward filtering
- Reality: 1:1.67 R/R is OK but pros aim for 1:2 minimum
- Better: Only trade setups with 1:2+ potential

❌ **My Issue #4:** No support/resistance analysis
- Reality: Professionals trade *at* levels, not random prices
- Better: Identify actual chart support/resistance, trade there

❌ **My Issue #5:** Ignoring Friday → Monday gaps
- Reality: Gap opens on Monday can invalidate Friday analysis
- Better: Wait for Monday open, then trade the actual technicals
